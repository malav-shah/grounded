What is this, exactly?

Real estate investing,
finally made simple

You don't need ₹1 crore to own real estate. REITs let you own a tiny slice of giant office parks — and earn quarterly distributions from them.

1
Big offices need many investors
Embassy, Mindspace and other REITs own massive Grade-A office parks rented by Google, JP Morgan, IBM. They pool money from thousands of investors like you to buy these buildings.
2
You buy tiny slices — called units
One unit of Embassy REIT costs around ₹380. That's your ownership stake in a 50 million sq ft portfolio of offices. Like owning a tiny fraction of a very big landlord.
3
Distributions credited every quarter
By law, REITs must pay 90% of their income to unitholders as quarterly distributions. Straight to your bank account — no effort required from you.
4
Sell anytime — it's listed on NSE
Unlike a flat that takes months to sell, REIT units trade on the stock exchange. You can sell in seconds, any trading day.

Common questions

Things people ask us

Is this safe? Who regulates it?
+
REITs are regulated by SEBI — the same body that regulates mutual funds and stock markets. Your investment is backed by real physical properties worth thousands of crores. It's not crypto, it's not a startup — it's regulated real estate.
What if I need my money back?
+
REITs are listed on NSE and BSE — you can sell your units any trading day, just like selling a stock or mutual fund. Unlike a flat, there's no buyer search, no registration, no months of waiting.
Do I actually receive distributions?
+
Yes. By law, REITs must distribute at least 90% of their income to unitholders as quarterly distributions. Embassy REIT paid ₹6.47 per unit in Q3 FY2026 alone — that's real money into your bank account.
Why start with ₹500/day?
+
Habit formation. The science of investing shows that consistent small investments beat large irregular ones. ₹500/day for a year = ₹1,82,500 invested. Small enough to start today, meaningful enough to grow into something real.
Which REITs will I invest in?
+
We'll start with India's 4 listed REITs — Embassy Office Parks, Mindspace, Brookfield, and Nexus Select Trust. These are backed by global institutional investors like Blackstone and Brookfield Asset Management.
How is this different from Groww or Zerodha?
+
Groww and Zerodha are horizontal platforms — they do everything. Grounded is built specifically for real estate investing, with education, portfolio tracking, and automation designed entirely around REITs. Think of us as the Jar for real estate.
